Differential Cyanosis

Hemodynamic simulation of neonatal congenital heart defects.

Normal Transitional Circulation

  • Anatomy: Structurally normal heart.
  • Pressures: Systemic (Aorta) > Pulmonary (PA).
  • Shunt: If PDA is open, flow is Left-to-Right (Red to Blue).
  • Outcome: Entire body receives oxygenated blood. SpO2 is equal in hand and foot.

PPHN (Persistent Pulmonary Hypertension)

  • Anatomy: Normal heart, but high lung resistance.
  • Pressures: Pulmonary (PA) > Systemic (Aorta).
  • Shunt: Right-to-Left via PDA. Deoxygenated blood enters the descending aorta.
  • Outcome: Differential Cyanosis.
    Upper body (Pre-ductal) = Pink.
    Lower body (Post-ductal) = Blue.

Critical Coarctation of Aorta

  • Anatomy: Severe narrowing of the aortic arch (pre-ductal).
  • Mechanism: LV cannot pump to lower body. Lower body depends on the RV pumping through the PDA.
  • Shunt: Right-to-Left via PDA (essential for survival).
  • Outcome: Differential Cyanosis.
    Right Hand = Pink (from LV).
    Feet = Blue/Grey (from RV).
